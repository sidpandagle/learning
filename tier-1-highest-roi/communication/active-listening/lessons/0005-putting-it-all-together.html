Lesson 4

Today's exercise plays out like a real meeting. You'll read a transcript, and at four decision points, choose the right move. Each decision point maps to one of the four skills.

The meeting

You're in a 1:1 with your manager, Alex. The subject is a project you've been leading. Read the transcript, then work through the decision points below.

1:1 — You & Alex — Tuesday, 10am
Alex I wanted to check in on the Orion project. I've been getting some signals from the stakeholder side that things feel a bit uncertain.
You [Decision Point 1 — see below]
Alex Yeah, exactly. It's not that they think you're doing a bad job — I don't think that. It's more that they're not sure what's coming next or when. There's a communication gap somewhere.
You [Decision Point 2 — see below]
Alex Right, it's less about the format — more that they feel like they only hear from you when something needs to be escalated. The proactive cadence isn't there. Anyway, I also wanted to flag the resourcing situation, which is a different issue. The team headcount request got pushed back again. I know that puts pressure on the timeline...
You [You zone out here — you start thinking about how to handle the headcount news. DRIFT]
Alex ...so I need you to think about what we can descope or delay without blowing the core commitment. I don't want to take this to the stakeholders as a problem — I want to go to them with a proposal.
You [Decision Point 3 — see below]
Alex Exactly. And the other thing I want to understand is whether you feel supported. I'm not always available and I know that's been a gap on my end too. I don't want you running into walls without a way through.
You [Decision Point 4 — see below]

Decision Points

Decision Point 1 — Catch the gap / Reflect
Alex just said stakeholders feel uncertain. This is the first thing he's said — you haven't responded yet.
What do you say?
Decision Point 2 — Ask the right question
Alex has clarified: it's a communication gap, not a performance concern. He said stakeholders only hear from you when something is being escalated — the proactive cadence isn't there.
What do you say next?
Decision Point 3 — Re-engage after drift
You zoned out when Alex mentioned headcount. Your anchor word was "resourcing / timeline pressure." Alex just finished saying: he wants a descope proposal to take to stakeholders, not a problem.
What do you do?
Decision Point 4 — Full loop
Alex has shifted tone — this is now about whether you feel supported. He admitted there's a gap on his end too. This is a human moment, not a project question.
What's the right move?

The one thing to do next

In your next real meeting: use the reflect-then-ask sequence once. Just once. Pick the most important moment, offer one reflection, then one question. That's it. Don't try to do all four skills at once — integrate one move at a time until it's natural.
