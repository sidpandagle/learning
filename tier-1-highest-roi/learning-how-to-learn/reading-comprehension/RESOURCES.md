# Resources: Reading Comprehension

## Primary Sources

- **How to Read a Book** — Mortimer Adler & Charles Van Doren (book)
  The classic on active reading. Introduces the four levels: elementary, inspectional, analytical, syntopical.

- **Make It Stick** — Brown, Roediger, McDaniel (book)
  Chapter on elaborative interrogation: asking "why" and "how" while reading dramatically improves retention.

- **SQ3R Method** — Francis P. Robinson (1941)
  Survey, Question, Read, Recite, Review. Well-evidenced pre-reading and active engagement framework.
  https://ucc.vt.edu/academic_support/study_skills_information/sq3r_reading-study_system.html

## Secondary Sources

- **The Learning Scientists — Elaborative Interrogation**
  https://www.learningscientists.org/elaborative-interrogation
  Short, research-grounded explainer.

- **Farnam Street — How to Read a Book**
  https://fs.blog/how-to-read-a-book/
  Practical distillation of Adler's framework with modern examples.
