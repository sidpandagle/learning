# Mission: Using AI Effectively

## Why
AI tools keep producing vague or off-target outputs, and the back-and-forth to fix them takes longer than doing the task manually. I'm also not reaching for AI consistently — I don't have a clear instinct for which tasks it's actually good for. The goal is to become someone who uses AI as a genuine force multiplier, not an occasional novelty.

## Success looks like
- Prompts that produce a useful result in one or two tries, not ten
- A clear personal framework for deciding when AI saves time vs wastes it
- A repeatable workflow where reaching for AI feels natural and intentional
- Knowing exactly what to fix when an output misses the mark

## Constraints
- Learning style: mixed (reading + interactive exercises + quizzes)
- Primary context: work tasks — writing, research, summarising, planning

## Out of scope
- Building AI products, APIs, or integrations
- Machine learning theory or model internals
- Safety, alignment, or policy debates
