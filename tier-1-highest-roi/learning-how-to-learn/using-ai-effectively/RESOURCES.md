# Resources: Using AI Effectively

## Prompt Engineering
- [OpenAI Prompt Engineering Guide](https://platform.openai.com/docs/guides/prompt-engineering) — Official best practices: clear instructions, context, few-shot examples, step-by-step reasoning
- [Anthropic Claude Prompting Guide](https://docs.anthropic.com/en/docs/build-with-claude/prompt-engineering/overview) — Anthropic's own guide to getting the best out of Claude: roles, context, chain-of-thought
- [Prompting Fundamentals — Learn Prompting](https://learnprompting.org/docs/basics/intro) — Free open-source course on prompting from first principles

## Practical AI Usage
- [One Useful Thing — Ethan Mollick](https://www.oneusefulthing.org) — Best practitioner writing on how to integrate AI into real knowledge work. Start with "Using AI Effectively"
- [Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI — Ethan Mollick](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/741805/co-intelligence-by-ethan-mollick/) — The definitive book on developing effective AI working habits
- [AI Explained — YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@aiexplained-official) — Clear explainers on model behaviour and capabilities without the hype

## Mental Models & Frameworks
- [Thinking About AI as an Intern — Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2023/Aug/3/weird-world-of-llms/) — The "brilliant intern" framing for understanding why AI needs explicit context
- [The Hallucination Problem — MIT Technology Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2023/05/02/1072528/geoffrey-hinton-ai-risks-climate-change/) — What hallucination is, why it happens, and how to manage it
- [How to Use AI to Do Stuff — Ethan Mollick](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/how-to-use-ai-to-do-stuff-an-opinionated) — Opinionated practical guide to which tasks AI handles well vs poorly

## Advanced
- [The 80% Problem — Ethan Mollick](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/the-80-problem) — Why AI gets you 80% of the way there and what to do about the last 20%
- [Chain-of-Thought Prompting — Google DeepMind](https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903) — Original research paper on why asking AI to "think step by step" improves accuracy
