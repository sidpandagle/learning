# Mission: Research Skills

## Why
Finding reliable information online is harder than it looks — most search results are noisy, biased, or low-quality, and it's hard to know which sources to trust. The goal is to become someone who can find the right information quickly, evaluate whether it's trustworthy, and act on it with confidence.

## Success looks like
- Reaching a reliable answer in 5–10 minutes on any question, not spending an hour in circles
- A clear personal checklist for judging whether a source is trustworthy
- Knowing exactly where to look for different types of questions (data, expert opinion, primary sources)
- Being able to synthesise multiple sources into a clear, confident view

## Constraints
- Primarily online research (web-based, not library or academic database focused)
- Practical focus — work tasks, learning, personal decisions

## Out of scope
- Academic research methodology or systematic reviews
- Investigative journalism techniques
