# Mission: Active Listening

## Why
In meetings, I consistently miss what people are actually trying to communicate — not because I lack attention, but because I'm interrupting, zoning out, or jumping to solutions before the other person finishes. I want to hear the real message, not just the words.

## Success looks like
- Catching the underlying concern or need behind what someone says in a meeting
- Sitting with what someone is saying long enough to respond to *their* point, not my reaction to it
- Noticing when I've started to zone out and being able to re-engage in the moment
- Asking questions that open up the conversation rather than redirect it to my agenda

## Constraints
- Learning style: mixed (reading + interactive exercises + quizzes)
- Primary context: professional meetings

## Out of scope
- Empathic listening for therapy or grief contexts
- Active listening in large audiences or lectures
- Negotiation tactics (covered separately)
