# Reflective Listening — Paraphrase Before You Respond

User completed Lesson 2. The core skill: before responding to anything in a meeting, offer one sentence that paraphrases what the speaker meant — not what they said verbatim, and not a preamble to your own point.

Key distinctions now established:
- **Content reflection** captures the factual meaning; **emotion reflection** captures the feeling underneath. Both are valid; content is the safer starting point in professional contexts.
- A reflection is not parroting (copying words), not agreeing (taking a position), and not a soft intro to your own argument.
- The payoff of reflecting is space — the speaker either confirms you understood, or corrects you, which surfaces what they actually meant.

**Implications:** User knows the *what* and *why* of reflective listening, and has practiced it against 4 realistic meeting scenarios. Ready for Lesson 3: what to say *after* the reflection — asking questions that open a conversation vs. questions that redirect it back to your agenda.
