## What was covered

- **Cognitive surplus model**: Speaking pace (~125 WPM) vs. processing capacity (400–500 WPM) leaves 275–375 WPM of spare mental capacity that the brain automatically fills with something.
- **Three gap-filling patterns**: The Interrupter (urgency to speak), The Zoner (unrelated thought), The Solution Jumper (pre-formed answer). User's mission context flags all three as real patterns for them.
- **Three levels of listening** (Co-Active model): Level 1 = internal (filtered through self), Level 2 = focused (on the speaker), Level 3 = global (the whole room/energy). Goal is to catch Level 1 and shift to Level 2.
- **Self-assessment quiz**: Identifies dominant pattern from 5 scenarios.

## Key insight

The gap isn't a character flaw — it's structural. This reframe matters because it shifts the question from "why can't I focus?" to "what am I filling the gap with, and is that useful?"

## Zone of proximal development going in to Lesson 2

User knows *what* goes wrong and *why* structurally. They do not yet have a repeatable technique for staying at Level 2. Ready for the first concrete skill: reflective listening.
